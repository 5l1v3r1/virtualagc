What is "The Restoration"?

"The Restoration" is an attempt to power up and run one of the original Apollo Guidance Computers.  I'm told (in 2019) that there is presently no other AGC in operation in the world.

  

Specifically, this is an attempt to operate one particular Block II AGC:

Part number: 2003100-071
Serial number:  (Raytheon) 14
Owner:  Jimmie Loocke

I have not been in contact with Jimmie (I think), but according to online information, he worked in Project Apollo as a thermal vacuum test technician, and was thus in a good position years later to recognize the AGC when he saw it in a warehouse of junk that was intended to be scrapped for recycling purposes.  After rescuing the AGC from its fate, the idea was eventually conceived of actually restoring it and operating it.


Jimmie Loocke (seated), Fred Haise (light blue shirt)
Jimmie Loocke (right), Charlie Duke (center)

While this AGC never flew a mission, the restoration team believes it was used in the vacuum testing of LTA-8 (Lunar Test Article), which seems like a reasonable hypothesis since G&N (Guidance & Navigation) system 602 did indeed use an AGC p/n 2003100-071, and we have no record of that AGC model having been used for any other purpose.  On the other hand, one of the circuit modules in the AGC is dated 1970, so if this AGC had been used in LTA-8, some of its modules were swapped out much later. 

By the way, LTA-8 used G&N system #602, of which we have the engineering drawings, including the drawings for AGC p/n 2003100-071.  By "engineering drawings", of course, I mean the mechanical drawings and the electrical schematics.  Other pertinent facts about G&N system #602 are that it would have used DSKY model 2003985-081 (whose drawings are also at the link just given) and that the AGC would have been loaded with the AURORA software.  We have a copy of AURORA 12, and you can view it in a variety of forms, including a pretty-printed transcription to source code.  Whether this is the exact version of the software is unclear, but it is a version that would undoubedly have functioned adequately in LTA-8.  A significant fact about AURORA is it includes a full suite of self-test software ... the last AGC software version to do so, since in later versions the self-test was greatly curtailed in order to save precious memory.  Thus AURORA was typically the software used during Project Apollo itself to check out the AGC.
